## The eight questions

**Question 1**
Which best describes how your business currently uses AI tools?
- We don't use any AI tools yet [1]
- We use one or two tools but mostly for basic tasks [2]
- We use several AI tools across different parts of the business [3]
- AI tools are integrated into most of our key workflows [4]

**Question 2**
How connected are your business tools to each other?
- Most things are separate — we copy information between systems manually [1]
- Some tools are connected but a lot still requires manual input [2]
- Most of our tools talk to each other automatically [3]
- Everything is integrated — data flows between systems without manual intervention [4]

**Question 3**
When a new lead or enquiry comes in, what happens?
- Someone on the team handles it manually when they get to it [1]
- We have a basic process but it's not always followed consistently [2]
- We have an automated acknowledgement but follow-up is still manual [3]
- The entire follow-up sequence is automated until a human needs to step in [4]

**Question 4**
How much of your team's time goes to tasks that feel repetitive and manual — scheduling, chasing, data entry, follow-ups?
- More than half our time [1]
- Roughly a quarter to half our time [2]
- Less than a quarter — we've automated most of it [3]
- Almost none — our workflows are largely automated [4]

**Question 5**
Do you know what your current AI tools are costing you versus what they're producing?
- No — we don't track this [1]
- We have a rough sense but nothing precise [2]
- We track costs but not the revenue impact [3]
- Yes — we measure both cost and return on our AI investment [4]

**Question 6**
How would you describe your website's performance as a business tool?
- It's basically a brochure — it doesn't generate leads or do much for us [1]
- It generates some interest but conversion is low [2]
- It generates consistent leads that feed our pipeline [3]
- It's a core part of our revenue system — fully integrated with our CRM and workflows [4]

**Question 7**
When you've tried to implement new tools or automations in the past, what usually happens?
- We haven't really tried [1]
- We try but it doesn't stick — the team goes back to the old way [2]
- It works initially but breaks down over time without maintenance [3]
- New tools get adopted and integrated successfully most of the time [4]

**Question 8**
What's the most honest description of where your business is with AI right now?
- We know we should be doing something but haven't started [1]
- We've invested in tools but we're not getting the return we expected [2]
- We're getting some value but we know there's more on the table [3]
- AI is working well for us — we're looking to go deeper [4]

## Opening — no heading, flows from H1

Before AI integration, before digital strategy, before Maru Online — there was Maru Sponsorship and Marketing.

In 2002 Jimmy co-founded it from scratch. No clients, no revenue, no guaranteed contracts. Within a year he had personally negotiated a R10 million per annum title sponsorship for the South African Open Golf Championship with South African Airways — a deal that grew to R28 million per annum over seven years. By 2007 the business was generating over R70 million annually, holding commercial rights to one of South Africa's most prestigious sporting events.

He was not an advisor on that business. He built it, owned it, ran it, negotiated the deals himself, managed the relationships, delivered the events, and was personally accountable when things went wrong — including a two-year legal battle to recover a full contract value from a Dubai World subsidiary after the global financial crisis. He recovered every rand.

That background matters for one reason: Jimmy understands what it means to run a business where the outcomes are real, the stakes are personal, and someone has to be in the room making it happen. That's the same standard he applies at Maru Online.

## Section — The pivot
### Heading: The pivot that became Maru Online

In 2014, Jimmy accompanied his wife to Barcelona while she completed an MBA at the European University. EU labour regulations prevented formal employment, but a Barcelona-based software company made serious efforts to bring him on — efforts that planted a seed.

He spent 18 months teaching business English to executives at IBM Spain, Adecco and G4S Security while watching European businesses run rings around South African competitors in one specific area: their ability to connect their systems, automate their workflows, and measure what was actually working.

When he returned to South Africa in 2016, he brought that observation with him. Most SA SMEs had the same commercial instincts and relationship-driven approach that had always characterised the market — but they were leaving significant revenue on the table because their operations weren't connected. The tools existed. The integration didn't.

That gap is what Maru Online was built to close.

## Section — How Jimmy works
### Heading: How Jimmy works — and why it's different

Two decades of commercial experience produce one instinct above all others: diagnose before you prescribe.

Every Maru engagement starts with a structured diagnostic — not because it's a billable first step, but because building the wrong thing faster is still building the wrong thing. Jimmy has seen what happens when consultants arrive with a preferred solution and retrofit the problem to fit it. He's been on the receiving end of that approach. Maru doesn't work that way.

The diagnostic finds what's actually broken. The implementation fixes specifically that. The measurement phase 30 days later shows you the numbers that prove it worked — or tells you honestly what needs adjusting.

He works with a maximum of five clients at any time. Not as a positioning statement — as a practical commitment to doing the work properly. Every client gets his full attention. Every engagement is scoped to deliver something specific and measurable. Every invoice reflects work completed, not hours logged.

## Section — The sectors
### Heading: What ten years of watching SA businesses operate leaves you

Having worked across South African sport, entertainment, hospitality, digital and now AI integration — Jimmy brings something most consultants don't: he has watched SA SMEs operate in real conditions. Load shedding, late payments, relationship-driven sales cycles, lean teams doing the work of three departments.

He knows that an AI workflow that works in a US case study doesn't automatically translate to a Johannesburg medico legal practice or a conference organiser managing 400 attendees with two staff members. The diagnostic frameworks he uses are built around how SA businesses actually operate — not how a global platform assumes they do.

Current sector focus: medico legal appointment scheduling, HR and recruitment, conference and events. If your sector isn't listed, get in touch. The diagnostic process applies broadly.
